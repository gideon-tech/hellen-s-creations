Our Story

Hellen Creations was founded to address the gap between talented women artisans in Uganda and the global market. We saw incredible skill and creativity going unrecognized — and we set out to change that.

Our artisans create handcrafted crochet bags, beaded bags, prize bags, seedbead jewelry (necklaces, earrings, waist beads, and anklets), and carpets (after-drags) — each piece a testament to their skill and dedication.

What makes us different: we showcase the women making the crafts on our website. This proves to buyers that the craftspeople are the real makers — not a faceless middleman.

Each product sold contributes a fee back to Hellen Creations to fund further training, separate from the crafters' own pricing and shipment costs. Every purchase is an investment in a woman's future.

From a Rescue to a Revolution

"When I had nothing else, I had my hands and a vision."

The Roots of My Resilience

My name is Nyamwenge Saudah. I grew up in a home where resources were scarce and the family was vast — my father had over ten wives, and I was one of dozens of children navigating a life defined by having nothing. In that environment, you can easily feel invisible. But even when I had nothing else, I had my hands and a vision.

The Rescue in the Craft

Crafts rescued me. When the world felt heavy, beadwork and crochet were my peace. It wasn't just art; it was my way out. I started sharing my work on social media, hoping someone would see the value in what I could create. My aunt, Evlyn Schiller, saw my talent online and sponsored my education, allowing me to earn my Certificate in Fashion and Design. That one act of "seeing" me changed the trajectory of my entire life.

The Lesson I Learned in the Field

When I began working with NGOs as a trainer, I saw my own reflection in the eyes of women in stone quarries, single mothers, and survivors of domestic violence. I realized that while teaching a skill is beautiful, training alone is not enough. Without a market, a skill is just a hobby; with a market, it is freedom.

My Mission at 26: I am no longer just a survivor; I am a bridge. I founded this journey to ensure that no talented youth or hardworking mother stays overlooked the way I once felt. We are combining professional design with real-world market access. I know what it means to have nothing, and that is exactly why I won't stop until these women have everything they need to stand on their own.
