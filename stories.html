Stories & Gallery

Behind every handcrafted piece is a woman with a powerful story of resilience, creativity, and hope.

Voices of Our Artisans

These are the women behind Hellen Creations. Each one has transformed her life through the art of handcrafting.

From Hardship to Hope

"Hellen Creations gave me more than a skill — it gave me my dignity back."

Many women in rural Uganda face limited opportunities. Through Hellen Creations' training programmes, women who once struggled to provide for their families have found a path forward. They learn to weave, bead, and craft products that are sold locally and internationally, earning a sustainable income that transforms not just their lives, but their entire communities.

Strength in Sisterhood

"When we work together, we lift each other up. No one is left behind."

The women of Hellen Creations don't just share a workspace — they share their lives. They mentor one another, pool resources during hard times, and celebrate each other's victories. This sisterhood has become a powerful support network, giving women the courage to dream bigger and reach further than they ever thought possible.

Crafting a Better Future

"My children can go to school now. That is everything."

For many artisans, the income from their craft has meant the difference between their children attending school or staying home. Education is the greatest gift these women can give, and through their skilled hands, they are breaking the cycle of poverty one family at a time. Every product purchased directly supports a mother's dream for her children's future.

Skills That Last a Lifetime

"I came here with nothing. Now I can teach others what I know."

Hellen Creations' training goes beyond basic craftsmanship. Women learn business skills, financial literacy, and product design. Many have gone on to train others in their villages, creating a ripple effect of empowerment that extends far beyond the workshop walls. What begins as a single woman learning to craft becomes a movement of change across communities.

Have You Seen Something Beautiful Today?

"I want to buy my mum a house once I learn well."

This is Faith, a 16-year-old girl who was affected by malaria which impacted her mental health and caused a disability. Two years ago, she almost lost her life in a fire — despite everything turning to ashes, Faith survived.

She is so loving, and together with her best friend, we are training them to make small handcrafted objects — both for their mental health and relaxation, and for sale. Faith dreams of buying her mum a house, because her mum struggles with medical bills and poverty just to make sure her daughter is okay.

Her mum says she has never seen Faith this happy and focused. She used to escape from home, but ever since Hellen Creations entered her life, things changed. Once they have mastered the crafts, Faith's products and her friend's will be featured on our website to support her dream — and to prove that disability is never a barrier to achieving your dreams.

Surprisingly, she is great with numbers and English. We love you, Faith — you are never alone, and the world is here for you.
